What is Trigonometry?

Trigonometry is a branch of mathematics that studies relationships between the sides and angles of triangles. It's widely used in engineering, physics, astronomy, and many other fields.

Trigonometric Ratios

These ratios are foundational to trigonometry. The table below summarizes the key relationships:

Ratio Formula Description
Sine (sin) Opposite / Hypotenuse Measures the height of the angle's opposite side.
Cosine (cos) Adjacent / Hypotenuse Measures the base of the angle's adjacent side.
Tangent (tan) Opposite / Adjacent Ratio of the height to the base of the triangle.
Cotangent (cot) Adjacent / Opposite Inverse of tangent.
Secant (sec) Hypotenuse / Adjacent Inverse of cosine.
Cosecant (csc) Hypotenuse / Opposite Inverse of sine.

Real-Life Applications

  • Architecture: Designing structures and calculating load forces.
  • Astronomy: Measuring distances between stars and planets.
  • Engineering: Analyzing mechanical systems and wave patterns.
